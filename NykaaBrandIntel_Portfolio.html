Project Overview

What This Project Does

A comprehensive brand management intelligence dashboard built entirely in Power BI, using real India-specific data from Nykaa and Google Trends to identify the next high-opportunity beauty sub-category.

The Business Question

As a Brand Manager at an Indian e-commerce giant entering the beauty vertical, which sub-category should you focus on? Where are the gaps between consumer demand and current supply? Which brands dominate — and where is there room to enter?

This project answers those questions with data, not opinion.

Project Objectives

  • Identify the highest-opportunity beauty sub-category using a composite scoring model
  • Map brand competitive positioning across rating, discount depth, and review volume
  • Correlate Google search demand trends with actual platform supply gaps
  • Assess margin health across sub-categories using discount depth as a proxy
  • Deliver a strategic entry recommendation with a 12-month GTM roadmap
Dataset 1 · Nykaa India
Nykaa Popular Brands & Products (2022)
~6,800 products across Hair Care, Skin Care, Makeup, Fragrance, Bath & Body, Men's Grooming. Includes brand, price, MRP, discount %, rating, and review count.
Dataset 2 · Nykaa India
Nykaa Product Reviews Dataset
~10,000 individual product reviews with rating score, review text, verified purchase flag, and review date. Used for sentiment analysis and trend mapping.
Dataset 3 · Google Trends India
Beauty Sub-Category Search Index (2020–2025)
5-year weekly search index for 6 sub-categories — Face Serum, Hair Serum, Sunscreen, Lipstick, Face Wash, Hair Oil — filtered specifically for India.

Key Findings

What the Data Revealed

Four headline findings that drive the strategic recommendation.

01
Search Demand
Face Serum is India's fastest-growing beauty search term
Google Trends India shows Face Serum search volume grew 3x between 2020 and 2024 — the steepest acceleration of any beauty sub-category. Yet platform product listings remain 40% lower than Face Wash, signalling a supply-demand gap.
02
Brand Positioning
Minimalist leads on quality rate despite being 5x smaller than L'Oreal
Minimalist holds the highest Quality Rate (87%+ products rated 4 stars or above) with a fraction of L'Oreal's SKU count — demonstrating that focused D2C positioning beats breadth in the premium skincare segment.
03
Margin Health
Hair Care shows the healthiest margin profile on the platform
Hair Serums and Hair Oils average below 15% discount depth — the lowest of all sub-categories — suggesting consumers are willing to pay MRP for quality hair care products. This contrasts sharply with Face Wash, which averages 28% discount.
04
Price Architecture
The Rs.300–700 band is under-served in Face Serum
The premium (Rs.500–1000) price tier accounts for only 22% of Face Serum listings but drives 61% of total reviews — consumers are actively seeking and buying premium serums. The mid-range band represents the highest-opportunity entry point.
Strategic Output

The Recommendation

Three prioritized sub-category bets ranked by the Brand Opportunity Index.

Rank 01 · Highest BOI Score
Face Serum
Fastest search growth. Lowest seller density relative to demand. Premium price point with low discount dependency. Minimalist and Dot & Key have validated consumer willingness to pay Rs.400–900. White space exists below Rs.500.
Rank 02 · Strong Growth Signal
Hair Serum
Second-highest value score. Healthiest margin profile on the platform. Rising search demand with strong seasonal pattern in Q4. Mamaearth leads but with moderate ratings — room for a quality challenger brand.
Rank 03 · Monitor Closely
Sunscreen SPF
Fastest absolute review growth in 2023. Regulatory tailwind from dermatologist endorsement trend. Currently underpenetrated outside metros. High execution complexity but long-term platform dominance potential.
